# Evaluation Rubric (1-5)

Use this rubric for consistent scoring and feedback.

## Score Meaning

- 1: No practical evidence, mostly theoretical or incorrect.
- 2: Basic understanding, weak ownership, unclear trade-offs.
- 3: Solid implementation experience, some measurable outcomes.
- 4: Strong ownership, clear trade-offs, data-backed decisions.
- 5: Senior-level depth, proactive risk control, system-level thinking.

## Dimensions

## 1) Project Ownership

- 1-2: Cannot clearly explain own scope or contributions.
- 3: Explains module ownership and execution details.
- 4-5: Demonstrates leadership in design, delivery, and incident handling.

## 2) System Design

- 1-2: Gives generic architecture with little rationale.
- 3: Provides coherent design with partial trade-off analysis.
- 4-5: Anticipates bottlenecks, failure modes, and growth paths.

## 3) Performance and Concurrency

- 1-2: Optimization statements are vague and not measurable.
- 3: Identifies bottlenecks and applies standard tuning.
- 4-5: Uses profiling evidence, concurrency control, and stable gains.

## 4) Database and Consistency

- 1-2: Weak index/model understanding and consistency reasoning.
- 3: Correctly applies indexing, transactions, and cache patterns.
- 4-5: Handles distributed consistency and schema evolution robustly.

## 5) Security and Reliability

- 1-2: Security/availability treated as checklist only.
- 3: Covers common controls and basic operation practices.
- 4-5: Designs preventive controls, observability, and incident playbooks.

## 6) Communication and Trade-Off Reasoning

- 1-2: Disorganized answers, little decision reasoning.
- 3: Communicates clearly with some trade-off detail.
- 4-5: Structured, concise, and decision-oriented under pressure.

## Recommendation Mapping

- average >= 4.5 and no dimension below 4: `strong hire`
- average >= 4.0 and no dimension below 3: `hire`
- average >= 3.3: `lean hire`
- average >= 2.7: `lean no hire`
- otherwise: `no hire`
